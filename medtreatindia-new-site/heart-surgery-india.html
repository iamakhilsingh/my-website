Procedures

Heart procedures we help patients plan

The right procedure depends on your diagnosis; a surgeon confirms the plan after reviewing your reports.

Coronary artery bypass (CABG)

Bypass surgery restores blood flow by grafting a healthy vessel around blocked coronary arteries. It is the most common cardiac surgery for international patients, usually for multi-vessel disease. Both on-pump and beating-heart (off-pump) CABG are performed routinely.

Heart valve repair & replacement

For damaged aortic or mitral valves, hospitals offer mechanical and tissue valve replacement, valve repair, and — for eligible patients who cannot undergo open surgery — TAVI/TAVR through a catheter, at a higher package cost.

Angioplasty & stenting

A non-surgical option for suitable blockages: a stent is placed through a catheter, usually with a short hospital stay. Many patients travel when local stent costs are high or drug-eluting stents are hard to access.

Pediatric & congenital surgery

ASD/VSD closure, Tetralogy of Fallot correction and other congenital procedures are performed at dedicated pediatric cardiac units — a common reason families travel to India.

How to compare hospitals for heart surgery

Hospital options may include Medanta, Apollo Hospitals, Max Healthcare, Fortis and Yatharth, depending on the patient's reports and needs. Compare the surgeon's experience with the proposed procedure, current hospital accreditation, cardiac unit capabilities, written package inclusions, language support and nearby accommodation before deciding.

Safety

Planning heart surgery and travel safely

Suitability, risks and recovery needs are individual. Ask the treating cardiac team for advice based on the patient's reports and health before making travel decisions.

Check accreditation

Verify the hospital's current NABH or JCI accreditation directly with the accrediting organisation.

Get the opinion first

Do not buy flights before a surgeon has reviewed the reports and confirmed that travel and the proposed procedure are appropriate.

Ask for everything in writing

Request the proposed procedure, package inclusions, implant or graft details, surgeon's name and possible additional costs.

Confirm fitness to fly

Ask the treating surgeon when the patient can fly and what follow-up or assistance may be needed during the return journey.

Frequently asked questions

Heart Surgery in India FAQ

Short answers for international patients comparing treatment options in India.

How much does heart bypass surgery cost in India?

An early planning range may be around USD 4,500 to 8,000, but the final cost depends on the reports, procedure, hospital, room category and package inclusions. Ask for a written, personalised hospital quote before travelling.

How long do I need to stay in India?

The treating team should advise the hospital stay, nearby recovery period and fitness-to-fly timing for the individual patient. Do not book the return flight until that plan is clear.

Can my family travel with me?

An accompanying caregiver may be eligible for a medical attendant visa, subject to current Indian visa rules and documentation. Confirm the requirements before booking travel.

What reports should I send for an opinion?

The hospital may request coronary angiography, an echocardiogram, ECG, recent blood tests, current prescriptions and other reports relevant to the diagnosis.

Does value-for-money mean the cheapest hospital?

No. Compare hospital quality, specialist fit, treatment scope, written inclusions and practical cost expectations instead of price alone.

What if the surgeon says I do not need surgery?

The specialist may recommend medication, monitoring or another procedure instead. Treatment decisions should follow the advice of a licensed clinician who has reviewed the patient's case.
