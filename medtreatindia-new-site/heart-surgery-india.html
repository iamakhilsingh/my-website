Cost planning

How much does heart surgery cost in India?

Indicative 2026 ranges for international patients at accredited private hospitals.

For international patients, heart surgery in India generally costs between USD 4,500 and USD 9,500 for most major procedures at accredited private hospitals — often a fraction of typical US private-hospital costs, as the table below shows. Lower cost does not mean lower standards: India's leading cardiac centres perform very high volumes of open-heart surgery each year, and high surgical volume is one of the stronger indicators of experienced teams.

Procedures

Heart procedures we help patients plan

The right procedure depends on your diagnosis; a surgeon confirms the plan after reviewing your reports.

Coronary artery bypass (CABG)

Bypass surgery restores blood flow by grafting a healthy vessel around blocked coronary arteries. It is the most common cardiac surgery for international patients, usually for multi-vessel disease. Both on-pump and beating-heart (off-pump) CABG are performed routinely.

Heart valve repair & replacement

For damaged aortic or mitral valves, hospitals offer mechanical and tissue valve replacement, valve repair, and — for eligible patients who cannot undergo open surgery — TAVI/TAVR through a catheter, at a higher package cost.

Angioplasty & stenting

A non-surgical option for suitable blockages: a stent is placed through a catheter, usually with a short hospital stay. Many patients travel when local stent costs are high or drug-eluting stents are hard to access.

Pediatric & congenital surgery

ASD/VSD closure, Tetralogy of Fallot correction and other congenital procedures are performed at dedicated pediatric cardiac units — a common reason families travel to India.

Best hospitals for heart surgery in India

We coordinate with NABH and JCI-accredited cardiac centres, primarily in Delhi NCR, including Medanta, Apollo Hospitals, Max Healthcare, Fortis and Yatharth. Rather than pushing one hospital, we compare two to three suitable options for your specific case on the surgeon's experience with your exact procedure, the cardiac unit's volume, package cost and inclusions, and practical factors like language support and nearby stay.

Safety

Is heart surgery in India safe?

India's top cardiac hospitals operate at very high volumes with internationally trained teams. To travel safely, keep these points in mind.

Insist on accreditation

Choose NABH or JCI-accredited hospitals — every hospital we work with holds one or both.

Get the opinion first

Never buy flights before a surgeon has reviewed your reports and confirmed you are a suitable candidate.

Ask for everything in writing

The procedure, package inclusions, implant or graft brands, and the surgeon's name.

Plan enough recovery time

Flying too early after open-heart surgery is a common avoidable risk — budget the full 2–3 weeks.

Frequently asked questions

Heart Surgery in India FAQ

Short answers for international patients comparing treatment options in India.

How much does heart bypass surgery cost in India?

CABG typically costs around USD 4,500 to 8,000 for international patients at accredited hospitals, depending on the hospital, room category and whether it is on-pump or off-pump. You receive a written, personalised quote before travelling.

How long do I need to stay in India?

Plan 2 to 3 weeks for bypass or valve surgery: 7 to 10 days in hospital and about a week of nearby recovery before a fitness-to-fly review. Angioplasty usually needs only 5 to 7 days.

Can my family travel with me?

Yes. India's medical visa route allows accompanying attendants on medical attendant visas, and attendant stay options can be included in your travel plan.

What reports should I send for an opinion?

Ideally coronary angiography, an echocardiogram, ECG, recent blood tests and your current prescriptions. Clear photos of paper reports are fine to begin with.

Does value-for-money mean the cheapest hospital?

No. Value-for-money planning focuses on a sensible balance between hospital quality, specialist fit, treatment scope and practical cost expectations.

What if the surgeon says I do not need surgery?

That can happen and is a good outcome. Some patients are better managed with medication or angioplasty. The aim is to share what the specialists advise honestly, even if it means not travelling.
