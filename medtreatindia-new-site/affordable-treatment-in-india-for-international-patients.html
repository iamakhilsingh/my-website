Affordable Treatment in India for International Patients: A Trusted Healthcare Option

Understand why India is a trusted healthcare destination for international patients seeking quality care, clearer cost planning and practical support.

Published July 8, 2026

Getting medical treatment in another country can feel overwhelming. Patients and families often have many questions: Which hospital should we choose? Which doctor can we trust? How much will the treatment cost? Who will guide us after we arrive?

For many international patients, India has become a trusted destination for quality medical care at affordable costs. The country offers advanced hospitals, experienced doctors, modern technology and a wide range of treatments across specialties such as cardiac care, orthopaedics, oncology, neurology, organ transplants, fertility treatment, dental care and wellness services.

According to the Press Information Bureau, India recorded 507,244 foreign nationals arriving for medical treatment in 2025, and India's medical tourism market was estimated at around USD 8.7 billion in 2025, with projected growth in the coming years.

Why International Patients Choose India for Medical Treatment

One of the biggest reasons patients travel to India is affordability. In many countries, the cost of surgery, consultation, diagnostics, hospital stay and post-treatment care can be very high. In India, many treatments are available at a much lower cost compared to several developed countries, while still giving patients access to experienced doctors and advanced medical facilities.

But affordability is not the only reason. International patients also choose India because of:

  • Highly experienced doctors and surgeons
  • Modern hospitals with advanced medical technology
  • Availability of multiple specialties under one healthcare system
  • English-speaking medical teams in many hospitals
  • Faster access to consultations, diagnostics and treatment planning
  • Support for medical visa, travel, accommodation and hospital coordination

India's healthcare system is also supported by quality standards such as NABH accreditation, and several hospitals in India follow internationally recognised healthcare standards. The Government of India notes that NABH-accredited hospitals follow patient safety and quality-care benchmarks.

What Makes Treatment in India More Affordable?

Affordable treatment in India does not mean low-quality treatment. The lower cost is often because of India's overall healthcare economics, skilled medical workforce, competitive hospital pricing and availability of high-quality medical infrastructure at comparatively lower operational costs.

For international patients, the total treatment budget may include:

  • Doctor consultation
  • Medical report review
  • Diagnostic tests
  • Surgery or procedure cost
  • Hospital room category
  • Medicines and consumables
  • ICU or recovery stay, if required
  • Follow-up consultation
  • Travel, visa, accommodation and local transport

The final cost always depends on the patient's diagnosis, medical reports, hospital choice, doctor's opinion, treatment plan, room type and length of stay. That is why patients should always get a proper medical opinion and estimated cost plan before travelling.

Common Treatments International Patients Travel to India For

India receives patients from different countries for many types of medical treatments. Some of the most common treatment categories include:

1. Cardiac Treatment

Patients travel to India for heart bypass surgery, angioplasty, valve replacement, pacemaker procedures and other cardiac treatments. India has experienced cardiac surgeons and hospitals equipped for complex heart care.

2. Orthopaedic Treatment

Knee replacement, hip replacement, spine surgery, sports injury treatment and fracture management are commonly sought by international patients. Orthopaedic treatment in India can be more affordable compared to many countries.

3. Cancer Treatment

India has hospitals offering chemotherapy, radiation therapy, surgical oncology, immunotherapy, targeted therapy and advanced cancer diagnostics. Treatment planning depends on cancer type, stage, reports and doctor evaluation.

4. Neurology and Neurosurgery

Patients may travel for brain tumour surgery, stroke-related care, spine disorders, epilepsy management, nerve conditions and other neurological treatments.

5. Organ Transplant and Advanced Procedures

India is known for advanced procedures including kidney transplant, liver transplant, bone marrow transplant and other complex treatments. These require careful eligibility checks, legal documentation, donor matching and hospital approval.

6. Fertility and IVF Treatment

Many international couples explore India for IVF and fertility care because of experienced specialists, advanced labs and comparatively affordable treatment packages.

7. Dental and Cosmetic Procedures

Dental implants, smile designing, cosmetic surgery, reconstructive surgery and aesthetic treatments are also popular among international patients.

Things to Check Before Travelling to India for Treatment

Before travelling for medical treatment, international patients should prepare carefully. Important steps include:

  • Collect all medical reports, scans, prescriptions and discharge summaries
  • Get a specialist opinion before travel
  • Ask for an estimated treatment cost
  • Understand the expected hospital stay and recovery period
  • Check visa requirements
  • Keep passport and travel documents ready
  • Plan accommodation near the hospital
  • Travel with a family member or caregiver, especially for major treatments
  • Keep emergency contact numbers available

A clear treatment plan helps patients avoid confusion and unnecessary delays after arriving in India.
